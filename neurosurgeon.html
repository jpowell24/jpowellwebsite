Why neurosurgery?

Overview

  • The basis of the medical field is solving problems that immediately improve someone's quality of life.
  • Neurosurgeon is preferred over neurology due to the directness of help provided.
    • Solving a problem with your own hands is more enticing to me than the iterative process of perscribing medications.
    • I am less interested in diagnosing patients, reading charts, etc., and more interested in providing needed help with relative immediacy.
  • The brain is everything. It is your memories, your personality, your creativity, consciousness, etc.
    • While many body systems are fascinating in their own right, the brain holds a mystic that demands lifelong attention.
  • Neurosurgery, in particular, also seems to have the largest variety of solvable problems.
    • For example: one may treat Parkinson's disease surgically using focused ultrasound or deep brain stimulation .
    • Who would have thought a degenartive disease could be treated further ablating neurons?

Why research in addition to surgery?

  • "Neurosurgery is the quickest specialty to use next generation equipment, and research is the only way to move the field forward." - Dr. Shih-Shan Lang Chen
  • Staying up to date and contributing to research makes you a better doctor, and corollary, a better researcher.
    • A surgeon will inherently have a better grasp on the technologies lacked in the field.
  • By design, medicine is becoming more about "following estabished protocols" as closely as possible---boring!
    • As time goes on, protocols get stricter and there becomes less and less room for "outside the box thinking."
    • Research provides a creative outlet outside of the operating room to keep the mind strong!
